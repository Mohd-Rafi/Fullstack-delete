Mechanization

Decreasing manpower needs.
Increasing earning power.

Over the years we have invested in technology, partnerships and engagement initiatives to enhance mechanisation in an industry that has traditionally had very high manual labour requirement. The non-availability of resident or migrant labour can often delay various agricultural operations. Adversely affecting sugarcane production, increasing costs and decreasing earnings. With programs like Mechanisation Melas and partnering leading mechanisation providers we are helping farmers get better yields and better profits. Farmer Training

To know is to grow.

One of the core values of DCM Shriramis to empower growers through continued learning and the adoption of newer agricultural practices for community and economic growth.

Some key strategies
we use are:
Big Kissan Gosthis:

Large gatherings involving as many as 700-800 farmers. Providing interactive sessions with research station agronomists and providing a forum for knowledge dissemination and cross-fertilisation of ideas.

Know More
Mobile Van Theatre (MVT):

Moving images that move people to action. Short films on various agronomic practices for sugarcane cultivation, saving water, soil health, safety precautions, and more.

Know More
Nukkad Natak:

Dramas with a theme depicting the importance and benefits of planting sugarcanes are regularly organized and connect with the hearts and minds of growers.

Know More
School Contact Program:

Making school children understand the importance of planting sugarcanes, who in turn go back and convey the message to their parents.

Know More
Mechanization Mela:

Conducted every year at one of our units. A forum for all major manufacturers of agricultural machines, tools, fertilizers, and pesticides to participate with over 1,000 farmers.
